A single leaf drifting in the wind, unknowing of its destination. For millennia he fled from himself, drifting around in this empty sector. For thousands of years, without purpose or direction, carried by the wind. The world had grown dull after hundreds of thousands of years. He remembered his younger days, when he dared to set his eyes upon Eternity, when he pointed his sword towards the Heavens within him, when he believed himself capable of overturning the Heavens and grasping Eternity. 
Back then he had been so confident, so arrogant and he paid the price for it. But he did not despair. If he could not grasp Eternity then perhaps a descendent of his could. And so he nurtured a clan. Under his supervision his descendants grew powerful and numerous. One day, while he was meditating, he felt a flash of providence unlike any other. Born to his clan was a man who's providence shone brighter than any star. On that day he declared that man his heir. 
That man was ambitious and he did nothing to curb those ambitions. Once again he grew overconfident, giving his heir free reign to whatever he wanted. After all how could a man so favored by universe itself die? In his hubris he saw no other possibilities, his heir would grasp Eternity and he could die happy knowing he has left a mark in annals of history. 
Even now he still could not understand how it could have possibly happened. That man was a beacon of prosperity and the clan flourished alongside him. But he had grown lax, he thought his clan invincible. He allowed his heir to delve into places no one should ever enter and uncover secrets that should never be uncovered. They managed to rip a hole into the Void, the nothingness between realities. And through that hole they called forth a beast that inhabited the emptiness. They though they could contain it, control it, or at least kill it, but they were so very wrong. He and his heir fought for as long as they could, but it was not enough. His entire clan was annihilated in the aftermath, and his heir killed. He only survived because an old friend arrived just in time to slay the beast.
Broken and without purpose, he fled the ruins of his clan. He found a remote corner of the universe and sealed his power, intending to live the rest of his life in relative peace. And so he did. He found a quant little planet lived there for the past few millennia. Sometimes he was a wandering merchant or sellsword, other times he was a simple farmer or hunter. Until now he was content in living this simple life till the end of his days.
"A leaf drifting in the wind, have I truly fallen so far?"
He look up towards the sky, a vibrant mix of violets and oranges. As the sun dipped below the horizon the orange fled and violet darkened to black. Lowering his eyes, he once again overserved the leaf drifting in the wind. He watched as it flew, free and unburdened, and with one final sigh, he let go.
